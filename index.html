About

Ernest Syu is a Singapore-based violinist known for his versatility as both a soloist and chamber musician. He is currently pursuing a Bachelor of Music in Violin Performance at the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory of Music, National University of Singapore, studying with Professor Qian Zhou.

Ernest has appeared as soloist with the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory Orchestra and the Academy of Taiwan Strings, performing major concertos including Tchaikovsky’s Violin Concerto and Mozart’s Violin Concerto No. 4. He is a prizewinner of multiple competitions, including First Prize at the Singapore National Piano and Violin Competition (Artist Category) and the Sylvia Shu-te Lee Violin Competition.

An active chamber musician, Ernest previously served as second violinist of the CAVE Quartet and concertmaster of the Sounds Good Chamber Orchestra in Taipei. His chamber collaborations include a performance of Chausson’s Concerto for Violin, Piano and String Quartet alongside violinist Paul Huang and pianist Ning An.

He performs on a 1726 Matteo Goffriller violin, generously loaned by the Rin Collection in Singapore.
