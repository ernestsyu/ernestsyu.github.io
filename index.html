Taiwanese violinist Ernest Syu is emerging as a dynamic and versatile artist, recognized for his expressive intensity, refined tone, and commanding stage presence. Equally active as soloist, chamber musician, and orchestral leader, he brings intellectual depth and emotional conviction to a wide-ranging repertoire.

Ernest first gained national recognition after winning First Prize at the National Piano & Violin Competition (Artist Category) in Singapore. He was subsequently awarded First Prize at the Sylvia Shu-te Lee Violin Competition and the Academy of Taiwan Strings Violin Competition, and was named winner of the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory Concerto Competition in 2025.

As a soloist, he has performed Tchaikovsky’s Violin Concerto with the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory Orchestra, as well as concertos by Mozart and Vivaldi in Taiwan and Singapore. His performances have been noted for their structural clarity and emotional sincerity.

A committed collaborator, Ernest has performed extensively in piano trio and string quartet settings, including major chamber repertoire by Beethoven, Ravel, Shostakovich, and Chausson. He previously served as Concertmaster of the Sounds Good Chamber Orchestra in Taipei and currently freelances regularly with the Singapore Symphony Orchestra.

He is currently pursuing his Bachelor of Music degree at the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory of Music under Professor Qian Zhou, where he continues to refine his artistic voice and expand his international performance profile.

Ernest performs on a 1726 Matteo Goffriller violin, generously loaned by the Rin Collection in Singapore.
