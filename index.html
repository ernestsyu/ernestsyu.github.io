ERNEST SYU

Violinist

“An assured and compelling performance, marked by expressive depth and confident command.”

— The Straits Times

“A performance of remarkable sincerity and musical maturity.”

— Pianofortephilia

Ernest Syu is a Taiwanese violinist known for his musical sincerity, stylistic versatility, and collaborative sensitivity. He is currently pursuing his Bachelor of Music degree at the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory of Music under Professor Qian Zhou.

He gained national recognition after winning First Prize at the National Piano and Violin Competition (Artist Category) in Singapore. In 2025, he was also named winner of the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory Concerto Competition. As a soloist, he has performed Tchaikovsky’s Violin Concerto, Mozart’s Violin Concerto No. 4, and Vivaldi’s Concerto for Four Violins with orchestra.

Beyond solo appearances, Ernest maintains an active presence in chamber music and orchestral performance. He previously served as Concertmaster of the Sounds Good Chamber Orchestra in Taipei and currently freelances regularly with the Singapore Symphony Orchestra.

He performs on a 1726 Matteo Goffriller violin, generously loaned by the Rin Collection in Singapore.
