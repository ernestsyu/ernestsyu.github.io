About

Ernest Syu is a versatile and dynamic violinist recognized for his diverse artistic voice as a soloist, chamber musician, and orchestral leader. He is currently pursuing his Bachelor of Music at the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory of Music, National University of Singapore, under the tutelage of Professor Qian Zhou. He regularly performs as a freelance violinist with the Singapore Symphony Orchestra.

Ernest first gained national recognition after winning First Prize at the National Music Competition (Taiwan) in both Piano Trio and String Quartet categories at the age of fourteen — a defining moment that affirmed his path toward a professional career in music.

As a concerto soloist, Ernest has performed Tchaikovsky’s Violin Concerto with the Yong Siew Toh Conservatory Orchestra and Mozart’s Violin Concerto No. 4 with the Academy of Taiwan Strings. His chamber music collaborations include a performance of Chausson’s Concerto for Violin, Piano and String Quartet alongside violinist Paul Huang and pianist Ning An.

An active chamber musician, Ernest has participated in international festivals including the Talis Festival Academy (Sarajevo), Keshet Eilon International Music Festival (Spain), Festival Academy Budapest, and Chofu International Music Festival (Japan).

He performs on a 1726 Matteo Goffriller violin, generously loaned by the Rin Collection in Singapore.
